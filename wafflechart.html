Waffle Chart

The waffle chart displays life expectancy data categorized into three ranges represented by different colors. The number of squares in each color indicates the count of years that fall into each life expectancy range. Yellow squares represent years with a life expectancy between 0 to 50 years. Orange squares represent years with a life expectancy between 51 to 70 years. Dark brown squares represent years with a life expectancy between 71 to 100 years. The distribution of colors across the chart provides a visual indication of how many years fell into each life expectancy range. The prevalence of dark brown squares suggests that for most of the years in the dataset, the life expectancy was between 71 to 100 years. This indicates a generally high life expectancy over the period represented. The few yellow squares indicate that there were very few years with a life expectancy of 50 or below. The orange squares show a moderate number of years with a life expectancy range of 51 to 70.
